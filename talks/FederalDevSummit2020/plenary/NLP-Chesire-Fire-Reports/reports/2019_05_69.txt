 Kitchen fire in Ellesmere Port Time of Incident: 21/05/2019 - 16:10 Location: Church Lane, Ellesmere Port Attendance: Two fire engines attended, one from Ellesmere Port and one from Powey Lane Shortly after 4pm firefighters were called to a report of smoke coming from the kitchen of a semi-detached house on Church Lane in Ellesmere Port. On arrival crews discovered a kitchen fire that is believed to have stemmed from food being cooked on a cooker hob having been left unattended. Everyone was accounted for and the gas and the electricity supplies were isolated. Two firefighters wearing breathing apparatus extinguished the fire with a hose reel jet. Once the fire was out a large fan was used to clear smoke from the property. They then used a thermal imaging camera to check for hotspots and make sure that the property was safe. Firefighters administered oxygen to a dog that lives at the address. The dog's owner showed symptoms of suffering from smoke inhalation. They were handed over to the care of paramedics. Firefighters were in attendance for less than an hour-and-a-half. For cooking safety tips visit https://www.cheshirefire.gov.uk/public-safety/home-safety/kitchen-fire-safety.
