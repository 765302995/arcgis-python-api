 Two-vehicle collision on the M6 Time of Incident: 12/11/2019 - 08:12 Location: M6 northbound, between junctions 21 and 21A Attendance: Two fire engines attended, one from Lymm and one from Warrington Shortly after 8.10am firefighters were called to reports of a car being on its side following a collision on the M6 northbound, between junctions 21 and 21A. On arrival the crews found that the collision involved a heavy goods vehicle and a small car. Nothing was on fire and no-one was trapped in the car, which was on its side on the hard shoulder. A slip road was closed by Highways England as emergency services worked at the scene. Firefighters assessed a female casualty, who was later handed over to the care of paramedics, and made the scene safe. Firefighters were in attendance for more than 15 minutes.
