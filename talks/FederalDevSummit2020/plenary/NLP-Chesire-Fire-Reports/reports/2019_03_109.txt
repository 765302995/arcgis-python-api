 Two-car collision on the M6 Time of Incident: 06/03/2019 - 17:19 Location: M6 northbound, between Junctions 20 and 21 Attendance: Four firefighters attended - one from Lymm, one from Stockton Heath, one from Warrington and one from Birchwood, Shortly after 5.15pm firefighters were called to reports of a collision just after Junction 21 on the M6 northbound. On arrival crews discovered that the collision near the Thelwall Viaduct involved two cars that were in lane four of the motorway.  No-one was trapped inside the vehicles and neither of them were on fire. Firefighters requested that Highways England and police attended to manage traffic. Lanes three and four of the motorway were fended off and Highways England later closed the motorway so that the vehicles involved in the collision could be recovered. Paramedics assessed the people involved in the collision as a precaution. Firefighters were in attendance for less than 45 minutes.
