 Wheelie bin fire that affected a domestic property in Willaston Time of Incident: 05/08/2018 - 13:36 Location: Crewe Road, Willaston Attendance: Two fire engines attended - from Nantwich and Crewe At around 1.35pm firefighters were called to reports of a wheelie bin being on fire next to a domestic property on Crewe Road in Willaston. On arrival crews discovered the bin well alight and that it was affecting the side of the building. The fire also affected the external electricity and gas supplies. A firefighter wearing breathing apparatus used a hose reel jet to extinguish the fire. Crews entered the property to see if the fire had spread. They used a thermal imaging camera to check for hotspots and found no signs of fire or smoke inside the building. Firefighters set up a cordon and evacuated the property next door due to a gas leak. The gas was isolated by a gas company while Electricity North West dealt with the electricity supply. Firefighters damped the areas affected by the fire down to make sure they were safe and rechecked for hotspots using the thermal imaging camera before leaving the scene. They were in attendance for around two hours and 45 minutes. For wheelie bin fire safety advice visit https://www.cheshirefire.gov.uk/public-safety/home-safety/wheelie-bin-fire-safety.
