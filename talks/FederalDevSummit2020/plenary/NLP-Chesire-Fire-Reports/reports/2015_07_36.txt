Building fire in Chester Time of Incident: 25/07/2015 - 11:58 Location: The Red Lion, Northgate Street, Chester Attendance: Two fire engines from Chester, one from Ellesmere Port, one from Deeside (north Wales) and an aerial appliance A kitchen fire at a public house in Chester spread into the roof space of the heritage building.  The fire involved a deep fat fryer in the first floor kitchen and firefighters wearing breathing apparatus used one hose reel to extinguish it. The blaze spread into the roof space and the crews used the aerial appliance to get access to the roof and remove tiles.  Once the fire was out the crews checked for hotspots using a thermal imaging camera.  A building officer was requested to attend the scene and the firefighters assisted with salvage from the building.
