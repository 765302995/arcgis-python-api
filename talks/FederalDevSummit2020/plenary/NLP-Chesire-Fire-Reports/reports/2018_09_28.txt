 Fire at a glass making building in Chester Time of Incident: 23/09/2018 - 23:57 Location: Ash Road, Chester Attendance: Three fire engines attended - one from Powey Lane, one from Ellesmere Port and one from Lymm - as well as a rope rescue unit from Lymm At around 11.55pm firefighters were called to a report of a fire at Quinn Glass Business Park on Ash Road, Elton, Chester. On arrival crews discovered a large fire involving the hot end of a glass bottle making plant had been put out by on-site personnel using dry powder extinguishers and on-site hose reel jets. A number people were suffering from the effects of smoke inhalation. They were handed over to the care of paramedics and three of them were taken to hospital as a precautionary measure. Firefighters checked the building, including the roof, for hot spots using a thermal imaging camera. A rope rescue unit from Lymm Fire Station was called to the building. A base was set up on the roof of the building and a firefighter wearing height safety equipment checked vents for hot spots. Water backpacks were used to cool areas with high readings on the thermal imaging camera. Once the building was deemed to be safe the incident was handed over to on-site personnel. Firefighters were in attendance for around three hours and 10 minutes.
