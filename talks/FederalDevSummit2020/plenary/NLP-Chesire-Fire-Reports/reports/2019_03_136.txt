 Minibus fire in Lymm Time of Incident: 28/02/2019 - 20:33 Location: Lymm services, M6 Attendance: One fire engine attended from Lymm Shortly after 8.30pm firefighters were called to a report of a fire involving a minibus at a car park at the Lymm services on the M6. On arrival the crew found that the fire had been extinguished by the nine-seater minibus' owner. The fire is believed to have been caused by an overheated catalytic converter. A firefighter wearing breathing apparatus used a hose reel jet to damp the vehicle down and make it safe. Firefighters also used a thermal imaging camera to check for hotspots. They were in attendance for around half-an-hour.
